Precision machining

Human hearing is remarkably sensitive. We can hear sounds as quiet as a mosquito buzzing near oneâ€™s ear and as loud as a jet engine. These extremes vary in amplitude by over six orders of magnitude. What elements of our inner ear enable such expansive discrimination?

In order to answer this question, Bartsch et al. designed and built a photonic force microscope (PFM). Similar to high-precision optical tweezers, the PFM was used to investigate if a particular protein called protocadherin15 could be one such element.

The PFM is theoretically capable of 3D-position measurements with high precision (less than 1 nanometer) and high bandwidth (up to 1 MegaHertz). In order to achieve those stringent benchmarks, all optomechanical components needed to be fabricated precisely and accurately.
