Precision machining

Hair cells are the sensory receptors of the ear that make hearing possible. Early microscopists who first discovered these cells named them so because of the cluster of cylindrical rods at the top of the cells called the hair bundle.

In 1990, Jacobs and Hudspeth painstakingly characterized the ultrastructure of hair cells so they could relate that structure to the processes of mechanoelectrical transduction and synaptic signaling. Using light and electron microscopy combined with computer modeling, they characterized the dimensions of hair bundles from a vestibular organ of the bullfrog known as the sacculus.

Guided by their measurements, I used computer-aided design to create a 3D-model of the hair bundle that captured and preserved its unique ultrastructure. I then printed that model for use in demonstrations and science outreach. The model was most notably seen in Jim Hudspeth’s TED talk entitled “The beautiful, mysterious science of how you hear”.
