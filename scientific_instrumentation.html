Scientific instrumentation

Scientific research evolves in response to new evidence and new personnel. As one researcher finishes their projects, new researchers join the lab with different skills and research questions.The scientific instrumentation in the lab must therefore adapt to the changing environment. As a research specialist in Jim Hudspethâ€™s Laboratory of Sensory Neuroscience, I ensure that those adaptations occur.

After a new research question is posed, I collaborated and worked with Dr. Hudspeth and the researchers to first define what tools are needed to answer their question. As demonstrated in the timeline below, I then used my experience in hardware, software, and fabrication and my meticulousness to create custom instrumentation.

2020

Cochlear Mechanics Study System

A precision system built to study the motion of the cochlea using optical coherence tomography (OCT). This setup allows for non-invasive, high-resolution imaging of cochlear mechanics.

2018

Custom Fabrication Tools

Designed and built custom tools to aid in the micro-fabrication of sensory tissue scaffolds, improving yield and reproducibility.

2016

Data Acquisition Suite

Developed a comprehensive software suite for simultaneous high-speed data acquisition from multiple sensory inputs, integrating hardware from various vendors.
