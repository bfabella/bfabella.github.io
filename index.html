I work in the Laboratory of Sensory Neuroscience, which studies the sensory transducers of the inner ear known as hair cells. Because commercial off-the-shelf solutions are typically not available, this research requires custom development of mechanical, optical, and electrical instrumentation systems. For the past 26 years, I have built and maintained such bespoke systems.

In order to further augment my capabilities, I recently obtained my Master of Science in Computer Science with specializations in machine learning and computational perception and robotics.

What I do

Using my experience, I integrate hardware from various manufacturers to create coherent instrumentation systems. Using computer-aided design (CAD) combined with subtractive and additive manufacturing, I build the custom components required by this specialized research. Finally, using software engineering, I develop the software to synchronize, control, and acquire data from the instrumentation systems; and I create programs that use signal-processing techniques, fitting algorithms, and dynamical-systems analysis to provide insight into the acquired data.
